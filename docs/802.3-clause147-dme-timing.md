# IEEE 802.3-2022 Clause 147 (10BASE-T1S) — DME timing extracts

Source: `/media/b-srv0/data/Media/Books/Standards/IEEE/802.3-2022.pdf`
(802.3cg content rolled into the 2022 revision). Extracted 2026-07-04 for the
spec-level oracle design (ticket 005). Page ~5908–5913 (Section Nine).

## 147.4.2 PMA Transmit — DME encoding rules

- `tx_sym` is a 5B symbol, encoded **LSB first**.
- A **clock transition** shall always be generated at the start of each bit.
- A **data transition** in the middle of a nominal bit period shall be
  generated iff the bit is a logical `1`; otherwise no transition until the
  next bit.
- Idle (`tx_sym = 'I'`, SILENCE): transmit one additional DME-encoded `0`
  (if the previous symbol was not `'I'`), then within 40 ns go high-Z
  (multidrop) or drive differential 0 V (point-to-point).

## Table 147–2 — DME timings

| Param | Description | Min | Nominal | Max | Unit |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| T1 | Delay between transmissions | 480 | — | — | ns |
| T2 | Clock transition to clock transition | −100 ppm | 80 | +100 ppm | ns |
| T3 | Clock transition to data transition (data = 1) | 38 | 40 | 42 | ns |

## 147.5.4.3 Transmitter timing jitter

> the maximum jitter at the transmitter side shall be less than **5 ns
> symbol-to-symbol** (test mode 1, fixture Figure 147–15).

## 147.4.3 PMA Receive (context for the RX direction)

- Receiver recovers clock+data from the DME stream; must achieve
  synchronization on both the DME stream and the 5B boundary within
  **800 ns** (10 bit-cells).
- When no line activity is detected, conveys `'I'` (SILENCE).

## Implications at PIO resolutions (analysis, not spec text)

One bit-cell = 80 ns. At the testbed's 8 cycles/cell (DME_H=4) a sys-clock
cycle maps to 10 ns; at a real RP2350 target of 150 MHz with clkdiv to
12.5 MBd, 12 cycles/cell → 6.67 ns/cycle. Both are **coarser than the spec's
edge freedoms**: T3's ±2 ns band and the 5 ns symbol-to-symbol jitter budget
are *sub-cycle*, and T2's ±100 ppm is a long-term rate accuracy (crystal
class), not per-edge slack. Consequences:

- Integer-cycle edge displacement is OUT of spec at these resolutions; a
  compliant PIO transmitter must hit the exact nominal integer grid.
- Fractional clkdiv on the bit clock (±1 sys-cycle delta-sigma wander) is out
  of spec at 150 MHz (6.67 ns > 5 ns) — only legal when the sys clock is fast
  enough that one cycle < 5 ns (> 200 MHz), with T2 rate met on average.
- The spec freedoms that survive at PIO resolution: **polarity** (DME decode
  is transition-based), **phase/startup latency** (bounded by framing, T1),
  the **schedule realization** (which instructions produce the edges, what
  clkdiv×program mapping yields the grid), and idle-line behavior.
